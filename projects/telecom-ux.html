UX/UI Work for Telecom

Enterprise B2B platform design for multi-location service management

Client: US-based telecom corporation (via CGI Inc.) Year: 2021-2023 Status: 🔒 Confidential / NDA

Note: This work is confidential and subject to NDA. Details below are limited to publicly shareable information. For a deeper discussion of this project, please reach out directly.

Overview

As the primary UX Content Designer at CGI Inc., I worked on complex B2B platforms for AT&T Business, supporting enterprise customers with multi-location service needs. This work involved designing content for high-stakes business tools used by large organizations to manage telecommunications services across multiple sites and departments.

The projects required close collaboration with UX Product Designers, engineers, and client stakeholders to create experiences that balanced technical complexity with business user needs.

My Role & Responsibilities

  • Designed and delivered UX content for complex B2B platforms supporting enterprise customers
  • Collaborated with UX Product Designers to develop high-fidelity wireframes for B2B division products and services
  • Partnered with engineers and client stakeholders to define information architecture and scalable content patterns
  • Translated dense technical, legal, and contractual requirements into clear, actionable UI language
  • Contributed to discovery and delivery through journey maps, personas, wireframes, and prototypes
  • Presented content strategy and rationale to cross-functional stakeholders, advocating for user comprehension while meeting business needs

Challenges & Approach

Complex B2B Requirements

Enterprise telecommunications platforms involve multiple stakeholders, intricate service hierarchies, and technical requirements that can overwhelm users. My approach focused on:

  • Breaking down complex technical concepts into digestible, actionable steps
  • Creating clear information hierarchies that matched user mental models
  • Establishing scalable content patterns that could adapt across different service types

Legal & Contractual Language

B2B platforms often require precise legal language while maintaining usability. I worked to:

  • Translate dense contractual requirements into user-friendly interface copy
  • Balance legal accuracy with user comprehension
  • Collaborate with legal teams to ensure compliance without sacrificing clarity

Multi-location Enterprise Needs

Supporting businesses with multiple locations required:

  • Content that scaled across different organizational structures
  • Clear role-based guidance for different user types
  • Flexible patterns that accommodated varying business complexities

Skills & Tools Applied

UX Writing Content Strategy Information Architecture Wireframing User Journey Mapping Stakeholder Communication B2B UX Enterprise Design

Key Learnings

  • B2B users need different patterns: Enterprise users often have different goals, constraints, and expertise levels than consumer users—content must accommodate varying technical knowledge while maintaining efficiency for power users
  • Stakeholder alignment is critical: B2B projects involve many voices—legal, compliance, sales, product, engineering. Clear communication and rationale are essential
  • Scalability matters from day one: Content patterns designed for one workflow will be applied to many—building flexible, reusable systems saves time and ensures consistency
  • Context is everything: Enterprise tools are often used in high-pressure, time-sensitive situations. Content must be scannable, precise, and task-oriented
